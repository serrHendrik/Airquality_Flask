Project Silk-Air

Since the dawn of the industrial age, we humans have tirelessly been increasing our impact on the environment. While deforestation and plastic waste in our oceans are side effects which can easily be observed, the pollution of our air is a different matter. As the criticallity of the situation became clear to the public by the effects of SMOG, governments have increased their interest and worldwide efforts to maintain control over air pollution are accelerating.

Modern infrastructures to gather data on fine dust and gasses face certain challenges. Most importantly, the equipment is expensive and stations remain at a fixed location. Measurements therefore are not always representative for the air which we breathe daily. Project Silk-Air explores a solution to this problem by turning air polution data gathering into a citizen science. To this end, we call upon the microcontroller community to step forth and lead the world into a better understanding of the quality of our air.
